# 📊 Sales, Marketing & Revenue Performance Analysis (Google Sheets)

## 📌 Business Context
This project analyzes e-commerce sales and profitability data to better understand how revenue is generated, how marketing and sales efforts translate into performance, and where opportunities exist to improve both growth and efficiency.

The objective is to connect data insights with real-world marketing, sales, and customer experience dynamics.

---

## 📊 Data Overview
The dataset spans 5 months, with approximately 5,000 records per month (~25,000 total rows).

It includes:
- Sales and profit data  
- Product categories: Technology, Office Supplies, Furniture  
- Customer segments: Consumer, Corporate, Home Office  
- Geographic performance (Region and State)  
- Order-level data  

This structure enables both cross-sectional analysis and time-based analysis.

---

## 🧹 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

### Data Cleaning
- Standardized date formats  
- Cleaned customer names  
- Standardized product categories and sub-categories  

---

### Feature Engineering
Additional variables were created to support deeper analysis:

- Month / Year  
- Customer_Lifetime_Value (CLV)  
- Customer_Segment  
- Profit_Ratio  
- Order_Size  
- Profit_Status  

These features enable more business-oriented and behavior-driven insights.

---

### Data Validation
- Error_Check column to flag:
  - Sales ≤ 0  
  - Missing customer names  

This ensures data reliability.

---

## 📈 Key Metrics
- Total Sales: $2,297,200.86  
- Total Profit: $286,397.02  
- Profit Margin: 12.03%  
- Total Orders: 5,009  
- Average Order Value (AOV): $458.61  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 💰 Revenue vs Profitability
- Strong revenue ($2.29M) vs lower profit ($286K)  
- Growth driven more by volume than margin efficiency

👉 High sales do not necessarily translate into strong business performance

---

### 💸 Discount Strategy Impact

- High discounts generate significant losses  
  → Profit: -$75,559 | Profit Ratio: -113.88%  

- Medium discounts are also unprofitable  
  → Profit: -$58,817 | Profit Ratio: -21.97%  

- Low discounts remain profitable  
  → Profit: $100,785 | Profit Ratio: 17.44%  

- No-discount orders generate the highest profitability  
  → Profit: $320,987 | Profit Ratio: 34.02%

👉 Profitability is largely driven by low or no-discount transactions

---

### 👥 Customer Segment Performance

- Medium segment drives the highest revenue ($1.29M)  
  → Profit Ratio: 11.49%  

- High-value segment generates the highest profit ($140K)  
  → Profit Ratio: 13.27%  

- Low segment contributes minimal revenue and profit

👉 Revenue is driven by mid-market customers, while profitability is more efficient in higher-value segments

---

### 💡 Key Combined Insight

The current strategy appears to rely on:
- discount-driven sales
- and mid-market customer volume

👉 However, this combination significantly limits profitability.

---

### 🌍 Market & Geographic Performance
- West and East outperform in sales  
- South and Central underperform  

👉 Suggests differences in targeting, channel effectiveness, or execution

---

### 🗺️ State-Level Observations
- Strong profitability in:
  - California
  - Massachusetts
  - Washington

- Texas shows strong revenue but weaker profitability  

---

### 📅 Time-Based Analysis
- Sales remain relatively stable across months  
- No strong short-term seasonal variation observed  

👉 Suggests consistent demand but potential lack of variation in marketing or external drivers  

---

## ⚠️ Interpretation

- The gap between revenue and profit indicates margin inefficiencies  
- Discounting strategy is a major driver of losses  
- Customer mix prioritizes volume over profitability  
- Regional differences suggest inconsistent execution

 It includes:

- Order-level data

---

## 🧹 Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

### Data Cleaning
- Standardized data formats
- Cleaned customers names

- Month / Year
- Customer_Lifetime_Value (CLV)

## 📈 Key Metrics
- **Total Sales**: $2,297,200.86

- **Profit Margin**: 12.03%  
- **Total Orders**: 5,009  
- **Average Order Value (AOV)**: $458.61  

---

## 📈 Analysis Approach
The analysis focuses on understanding:
- revenue vs profitability dynamics  
- product and category performance  
- regional and market differences  
- customer segmentation and distribution  

The approach is exploratory and aims to identify patterns that can inform marketing, sales, and customer strategy.

---

## 🔍 Insights

### 💰 Revenue vs Profitability
- Revenue is strong ($2.29M), but profit remains relatively low ($286K)  
- Growth appears to rely more on volume than on margin efficiency

👉 Key insight:  
High sales do not necessarily translate into strong business performance.

---

### 🖥️ Product & Offering Strategy
- Technology: highest profitability (~15.6%)  
- Office Supplies: stable (~13.8%)  
- Furniture: high revenue but very low profit (~3.9%)  

👉 Furniture behaves like a high-volume, low-margin category, which may indicate:
- aggressive discounting  
- misaligned pricing strategy  
- or products that are not well matched to customer expectations  

👉 Technology represents a high-value category with strong return on sales

---

### 🌍 Market & Channel Effectiveness (Regional Performance)
- West and East lead in sales  
- South and Central underperform  

👉 This may reflect differences in:
- campaign effectiveness  
- channel or media mix  
- brand visibility or positioning  
- market penetration  

---

### 🗺️ State-Level Observations
- Profitability is concentrated in California, Massachusetts, and Washington  
- Large markets such as Texas generate revenue but underperform in profit  

👉 This suggests potential gaps in:
- targeting strategy  
- product-market fit  
- or campaign effectiveness at the local level  

---

### 👥 Customer Segmentation
- Consumer: 64.2%  
- Corporate: 21.1%  
- Home Office: 14.8%  

👉 The business is primarily focused on mid-market customers, with less emphasis on higher-value segments  

👉 This may indicate:
- a volume-driven acquisition strategy  
- or missed opportunities in higher-margin customer segments  

---

## ⚠️ Interpretation & Marketing Perspective

These patterns suggest that performance differences may not only be driven by market conditions, but also by how marketing and sales strategies are executed.

Potential factors include:
- Campaign targeting: reaching audiences that are not fully aligned with the most profitable segments  
- Channel and media mix: investing in channels that generate volume but not high-quality customers  
- Product positioning: offerings that may not fully match customer needs in certain markets  
- Limited customer insights: insufficient data to accurately understand customer behavior and preferences  

---

## 🔎 Customer Experience & Lifecycle Perspective

Beyond acquisition, performance may also be influenced by how customers experience and interact with the business.

For example:
- Customers may not find the right products or offers  
- The overall experience (navigation, support, clarity of services) may impact satisfaction  
- Lack of follow-up or engagement may reduce repeat purchases  

👉 Even when a customer does not immediately convert, a positive experience can influence:
- future engagement  
- retention  
- brand perception  

This highlights the importance of:
- customer service  
- clear communication of services  
- consistent and trustworthy branding  

---

## 💡 Potential Actions (to be tested)

- Adjust pricing or discounting strategies in low-margin categories (e.g., Furniture)  
- Rebalance marketing efforts toward higher-performing categories (e.g., Technology)  
- Improve targeting to better reach higher-value customer segments  
- Reevaluate channel and media mix to focus on quality over volume  
- Strengthen customer engagement and lifecycle strategies (retention, repeat purchase)  
- Improve clarity and positioning of products and services  

---

## 🧪 Testing Approach

To validate these ideas, targeted experiments could include:
- testing pricing adjustments in low-margin categories  
- comparing performance across different channels or campaigns  
- evaluating the impact of improved targeting on customer quality  
- testing engagement strategies to increase repeat purchases  

---

## ⚖️ Business Considerations

Improving profitability may involve trade-offs:

- Reducing discounts could improve margins but impact sales volume  
- Focusing on higher-value customers could reduce total volume but increase profitability  
- Shifting channels may impact short-term performance while improving long-term efficiency
