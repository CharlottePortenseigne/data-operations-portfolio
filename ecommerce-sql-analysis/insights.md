# 📊 Detailed Insights – E-commerce Funnel & User Behavior Analysis

---

## 🚀 Project Summary

This analysis explores how users move through the e-commerce funnel and where conversion is lost.

👉 Key finding:

- **3,946 users viewed products**
- **948 users added items to cart (24.02%)**
- **144 users generated purchase events (3.44% conversion)**

Despite strong engagement, conversion remains low, revealing a gap between **user intent and purchase completion**.

---

## 📌 Business Context

E-commerce performance depends not only on traffic, but on how users move through:

- exploration (view)
- evaluation (cart)
- decision (purchase)

Each step reflects a shift in **confidence, perceived value, and trust**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Measure funnel performance
- Identify drop-off points
- Analyze user behavior and engagement
- Detect friction signals (cart, removal)
- Connect data to business and CRM strategy

---

## 📊 Data Overview

Dataset: Kaggle e-commerce event dataset

- **4,190 unique users**
- **5,898 sessions**
- ~**24,000+ events**

## 🔍 Key Insights

---

### 📊 1. Funnel Performance → Structural Conversion Issue

👉 Queries: 3–8  

The funnel reveals a major structural inefficiency:

- Only **24.02%** of users move from view to cart  
- Only **15.19%** of cart users convert  
- Final conversion is only **3.44%**

👉 Interpretation:

The issue is not user acquisition — users are present.

👉 The issue is **conversion**:
- users do not progress through the decision stages
- intent does not translate into action

---

### 🛒 2. Cart Stage → Highest Friction Point

👉 Queries: 17–20  

- **825 users** add items but never purchase  
- **55.80%** of cart users remove items  

👉 Interpretation:

The cart represents a **critical hesitation stage**:
- price sensitivity
- uncertainty
- lack of trust
- decision overload

👉 Business implication:
The cart is the **highest-impact optimization lever**.

---

### 👤 3. Engagement → Strong Predictor of Conversion

👉 Queries: 23–25  

- Non-buyers: **3.50 interactions**
- Buyers: **33.37 interactions**

Conversion increases with engagement:
- 1 interaction → **0%**
- 5+ interactions → **13.80%**

👉 Interpretation:

Conversion is not immediate.

👉 Users require:
- multiple exposures
- repeated evaluation
- progressive confidence building

---

### 💰 4. Revenue → Highly Concentrated

👉 Queries: 9–12  

- Total revenue: **8,361.89**
- Generated by only **144 users**

👉 Interpretation:

Revenue depends on a **small group of converters**.

👉 Business implication:
- improving conversion slightly could have large revenue impact

---

### 🏷️ 5. Brand Influence → Trust Signal

👉 Queries: 14–15  

Top brands:
- Runail → **700.25 revenue**
- Irisk → **649.56 revenue**

👉 Interpretation:

Brand acts as a **trust shortcut**:
- familiar brands reduce decision friction
- unknown brands may increase hesitation

---

## ⚠️ Data Interpretation Notes

👉 Query: 10, 21  

Important clarifications:

- **4.55** = average price per purchase event  
- NOT average order value per user  

- “Repeat purchasers”:
  - 133 repeat vs 11 one-time users  
  - reflects **event repetition**, not true retention  

👉 The dataset is **event-based**, not order-based.

## 🧠 Behavioral & Linguistic Interpretation (My Approach)

This analysis goes beyond traditional metrics.

👉 User actions can be interpreted as **intent signals**, similar to classification in NLP:

- View → information seeking  
- Cart → evaluation / hesitation  
- Purchase → decision threshold  

👉 The funnel reflects a **decision-making process**, not just clicks.

From a linguistic and cognitive perspective:
- users build meaning and value through interaction
- conversion happens when perceived value > perceived risk

# DETAILED INSIGHTS – E-commerce Funnel & User Behavior Analysis

- Viewers: 3,946  
- Cart: 948

---

## 👤 2. User Behavior

Session-level analysis shows that:

- Sessions with 1 interaction rarely convert  
- Sessions with 3–4 interactions show higher conversion rates  

→ Users require multiple interactions before purchasing.

- Purchases: 144

→ Strong hesitation before purchase.

---

Top-performing brands (e.g., Irisk, Runail) account for a large share of purchases.

→ Suggests that trust and familiarity influence user decisions.

## ⏱️ 6. Time Trends

---

## ⚠️ 7. Structural Issue

The data shows an **intent-to-conversion gap**:

- Only 144 complete a purchase  

→ Users engage but do not convert.

Users follow a decision process:

- exploration  
- comparison  
- hesitation  

→ Conversion depends on confidence, not just interaction.

## 🤝 9. CRM Perspective
