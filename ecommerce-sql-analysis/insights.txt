E-COMMERCE ANALYSIS – STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

--------------------------------------------------

1. Funnel Analysis

The funnel reveals two major drop-off points:
- View → Cart (~80%)
- Cart → Purchase (~87%)

This indicates that conversion issues occur both at the product engagement level and at the checkout stage.

This suggests a systemic issue rather than a single-point failure.

--------------------------------------------------

2. User Behavior

Sessions with only one interaction never convert.

Conversion increases with the number of interactions and peaks around 3–4 actions per session.

This reflects a progressive decision-making process where users:
- explore
- compare
- validate

before purchasing.

Engagement depth is therefore a key driver of conversion.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Marketing Insight

Traffic increases over time while purchases decrease.

This suggests that:
- traffic quality may be declining
- campaigns may be attracting low-intent users
- messaging may not align with user expectations

This highlights a gap between acquisition strategy and conversion performance.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Sales Insight

Conversion rates are low, but average order value remains relatively high.

This indicates that:
- users who convert are high-value customers
- revenue potential exists within current traffic

The main opportunity is to improve conversion rather than increase traffic.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Customer Experience (CX)

The largest drop-off occurs at checkout.

Possible friction points:
- unexpected costs (shipping, taxes)
- limited payment options
- long delivery times
- complex checkout process

This suggests that conversion loss is strongly linked to user experience.

--------------------------------------------------

6. Price Perception

Mid-priced products show the lowest conversion rate.

This suggests unclear positioning:
- low price = accessible
- high price = perceived quality
- mid price = ambiguous

User decisions are influenced more by perceived value than actual price.

--------------------------------------------------

7. Brand Influence

Some brands outperform others despite lower traffic.

This indicates that:
- trust
- positioning
- perceived quality

are stronger drivers of conversion than visibility alone.

--------------------------------------------------

8. Linguistic Insight

Category structure and product naming impact user behavior.

Complex or unclear labels increase cognitive load and reduce engagement.

Simplifying category structures improves interpretability and navigation.

This highlights the role of language as part of the user experience.

--------------------------------------------------

9. Strategic Synthesis

Conversion performance is driven by the interaction between:
- user intent
- engagement level
- perceived value
- user experience
- clarity of communication

Improving only one dimension is insufficient.

A holistic approach is required.

--------------------------------------------------

10. Potential Experiments

- A/B testing discounts on mid-priced products
- Simplifying checkout flow
- Testing different payment options
- Improving product descriptions and UX
- Refining targeting to focus on high-intent users

--------------------------------------------------

END OF INSIGHTS
