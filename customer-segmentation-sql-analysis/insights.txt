Customer analysis shows that purchasing behavior is highly concentrated among a small number of users.
Out of 144 purchasing users, 125 belong to the low-value segment, representing the majority of customers but contributing a smaller share of total revenue.
Only 18 users fall into the medium-value segment, while a single user qualifies as high-value, indicating that top customers are extremely rare.
Despite representing the majority of users, the low-value segment contributes the largest share of revenue (5,250.53), showing that volume compensates for low individual spending.
The medium-value segment contributes 2,739.52 in revenue, representing a smaller but still significant portion of total sales.
The high-value segment contributes 371.84, highlighting that even top customers do not dominate overall revenue in this dataset.
The average purchase value is 4.55, indicating relatively low transaction amounts and suggesting that users tend to make small purchases.
Overall, customer spending is distributed across many low-value users rather than driven by a small group of high-value customers.
